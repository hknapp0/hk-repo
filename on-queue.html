On Queue

When given the creative freedom to design our own app, my teammates and I came up with “On Queue”. Our live line tracking app was designed to alleviate the hassle and stress of waiting in line at an event.

View Clickable Prototype

The Problem

Waiting in line is sometimes inevitable and can be time consuming. Being late for an event can cause stress and anxiety. We observed that the market for this type of application is extremely undersaturated, even non-existent.

The Research

We started by conducting interviews, with the conclusions from our interviews we were able to group our findings in an affinity diagram and empathy map. After discovering more about our users, we created a proto-persona: Drew Ryan. Drew is an avid night-lifer living in Philly. From this persona we were able to create a user flow to inspire our earliest sketches of On Queue.

After we conducted extensive research, really putting ourselves in our users shoes and understanding their needs, we really took our design to the next level! We combined ideas from our sketches to create lo-fi wireframes. Upon gaining feedback from our lo-fi wireframes we designed our final wireframes, to create the final clickable prototype for On Queue.

The Solution

Create a tool that allows users to live track line wait times. This will allow users to better delegate their time before an event. Also, adding a feature that allows users to skip the line all together to combat the fear of missing out.
